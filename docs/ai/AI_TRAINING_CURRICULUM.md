## Training Overview

### Purpose

This training equips union stewards with the knowledge and skills to effectively use AI features in Union Eyes while understanding the responsible AI principles that protect members.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:

1. **Explain** what AI is and how it works in Union Eyes (basic understanding)
2. **Use** AI-powered features to improve case management efficiency
3. **Interpret** AI predictions and recommendations with appropriate skepticism
4. **Identify** when AI should NOT be trusted (limitations, bias risks)
5. **Apply** responsible AI principles in daily work
6. **Troubleshoot** common AI issues and know when to escalate

### Training Format

- **Duration:** 4 hours (can split into two 2-hour sessions if needed)
- **Delivery:** In-person preferred (virtual option available)
- **Group Size:** 15-20 participants ideal (max 25)
- **Materials:** Participant workbook, slides, demo system access
- **Assessment:** 20-question quiz at end (80% pass rate required)

### Learning Objectives

### Content Outline

**1.1 What is AI? (15 minutes)**

**Simple Definition:**
> "AI is a computer program that learns patterns from past data to make predictions or recommendations about new situations."

**Key Concepts (explain with union examples):**

- **Machine Learning:** Computer learns from examples (like a steward learning from past cases)
  - *Example:* AI reads 10,000 past grievances to learn what makes a case winnable

- **Predictions:** AI estimates what will happen (not crystal ball, educated guess)
  - *Example:* "This grievance has 85% chance of winning based on similar past cases"

- **Training Data:** Past cases AI learned from (quality matters!)
  - *Example:* If AI only learned from 2020-2023, it may not know about new employer tactics

- **Pattern Recognition:** AI finds similarities (like humans, but faster)
  - *Example:* AI notices "grievances with witness statements win 20% more often"

**What AI is NOT:**

- âŒ Not magic or perfect
- âŒ Not conscious or thinking like humans
- âŒ Not a replacement for steward judgment
- âŒ Not always right (can be biased or make mistakes)

**3.1 Why Responsible AI Matters (15 minutes)**

**Union Values & AI:**

- âœŠ **Solidarity:** AI should help ALL members fairly (no discrimination)
- âš–ï¸ **Justice:** AI must not perpetuate employer bias
- ğŸ›¡ï¸ **Protection:** Members' data must be private and secure

**Real Risks:**

1. **Algorithmic Bias:** AI learns from biased past data
   - *Example:* If past arbitrators were biased against women, AI may learn to predict women's grievances will lose (even if unfair)

2. **Privacy Breaches:** Sensitive member data could leak
   - *Example:* Medical info, disciplinary records, salary data

3. **Over-Reliance:** Stewards stop thinking critically
   - *Example:* AI says 95% win, steward doesn't investigate, misses fatal flaw

4. **Opacity:** AI is a "black box" (hard to explain decisions to members)
   - *Example:* Member asks "Why did AI say I'll lose?" â†’ Hard to explain

**Activity (5 min):** Brainstorm - "What could go wrong if we trust AI too much?"

**3.2 Detecting Bias in AI (20 minutes)**

**Types of Bias to Watch For:**

**1. Demographic Bias (most serious)**

- **Definition:** AI treats members differently based on race, gender, age, disability
- **Example:** AI predicts Black members' grievances will lose more often (even controlling for case facts)
- **Red Flags:**
  - Predictions consistently lower for certain demographic groups
  - "Similar cases" don't actually look similar (different race/gender)
  - Explanations mention demographic factors (SHOULD NEVER HAPPEN - report immediately!)

**2. Recency Bias**

- **Definition:** AI over-weights recent data, ignores long-term trends
- **Example:** If last 10 cases lost, AI predicts all future cases will lose (ignores 90% win rate historically)
- **Red Flags:**
  - Predictions shift dramatically after short losing streak
  - AI doesn't account for temporary factors (new employer lawyer, arbitrator on vacation)

**3. Employer Bias**

- **Definition:** AI learns employer's past success, assumes it will continue
- **Example:** If employer usually wins at Step 1, AI assumes they'll always win (ignores bad employer arguments)
- **Red Flags:**
  - AI over-predicts employer wins
  - Explanations cite "employer's past success" without analyzing current case merits

**4. Proxy Discrimination**

- **Definition:** AI uses factors correlated with demographics as proxies
- **Example:** AI uses "zip code" to predict outcomes â†’ zip code correlates with race â†’ indirect discrimination
- **Red Flags:**
  - Predictions vary by factors that shouldn't matter (location, shift, seniority in ways that correlate with demographics)

**How to Detect Bias (Practical Steps):**

1. **Compare Across Groups:** Track AI accuracy for different demographic groups
   - If AI is 90% accurate for white members but 75% for Black members â†’ BIAS

2. **Question Low Predictions:** When AI says low win probability, ask "Why?"
   - If explanation doesn't make sense or cites stereotypes â†’ DISTRUST

3. **Review Similar Cases:** Are they actually similar in relevant ways?
   - If "similar" cases differ by race/gender but AI claims similarity â†’ RED FLAG

4. **Report Suspicions:** If you suspect bias, report to Data Science team immediately
   - Email: <ai-fairness@unioneyes.org>
   - Don't ignore your gut - bias is serious!

**Case Study (10 min):**
> **Scenario:** AI predicts a 55% win probability for a sexual harassment grievance filed by a woman. Similar case (same contract violation, similar evidence) filed by a man last month got 78% prediction.
>
> **Questions:**
>
> 1. Is this potential bias? (YES - same facts, different prediction by gender)
> 2. What should steward do? (Report to AI team, investigate further, don't rely on AI for this case)
> 3. How should this be investigated? (Fairness audit, review all harassment cases by gender)

**3.4 Data Privacy & Security (10 minutes)**

**What Data Does AI See?**

- Case facts (grievance type, contract articles, evidence)
- Member demographics (for fairness monitoring ONLY - not used in predictions)
- Outcome data (win/lose, settlement amounts)
- CBA texts, policies, past arbitrator decisions

**What Data Does AI NOT See?**

- Member names (pseudonymized in training data)
- Social Security Numbers, bank accounts
- Personal health information (HIPAA protected)
- Communications with members (emails, messages)

**Your Responsibilities:**

1. **Don't input unnecessary PII:** When describing case, use "member" not names
2. **Secure your login:** AI access tied to your Union Eyes account
3. **Don't share screen with sensitive data:** In public, on Zoom calls
4. **Report suspicious activity:** Unauthorized access, data leaks

## Module 5: Q&A, Assessment & Certification (40 minutes)

### Content Outline

**5.1 Open Q&A (15 minutes)**

**Common Questions:**

**Q: Will AI replace stewards?**
A: Absolutely not. AI is a tool to make your job easier, not replace you. Members need human advocates with empathy, judgment, and union values. AI can't do that.

**Q: What if I don't trust AI?**
A: That's healthy skepticism! You don't have to use AI features if you're uncomfortable. They're optional tools. But understanding AI helps you evaluate it critically.

**Q: How often is AI retrained?**
A: Currently quarterly (every 3 months) or when significant drift detected. Future: automated retraining when accuracy drops.

**Q: Can employers access our AI predictions?**
A: No. Union Eyes is our system, not shared with employers. Employer has no access to our data or AI insights.

**Q: What happens to my data after a case closes?**
A: Case data retained for 7 years (regulatory requirement), then archived or deleted. Used to train AI only with pseudonymization (no names).

**Q: I found an error in AI prediction. What do I do?**
A: Great! Report it to <ai-support@unioneyes.org> with case ID and details. Helps us improve AI for everyone.
