# Employment Terms and Security Policy

**Policy Owner:** Chief Security Officer / HR Manager  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 12, 2026  
**Review Frequency:** Annual  
**ISO 27001:2022 Control:** A.6.2 - Terms and Conditions of Employment

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the security-related terms and conditions that must be included in all employment agreements, contractor agreements, and engagement letters at Union Eyes. It ensures that all personnel understand their security responsibilities, obligations regarding confidential information, and consequences of security violations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- Full-time employees
- Part-time employees
- Independent contractors
- Temporary workers and interns
- Third-party personnel with regular access to Union Eyes systems or data
- Board members and advisors with access to confidential information

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:
- ISO 27001:2022 (A.6.2 - Terms and Conditions of Employment)
- SOC 2 Type II (Trust Services Criteria - Security and Confidentiality)
- PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- Canadian employment standards legislation (federal and provincial)

## 4. Mandatory Security Clauses in Employment Agreements

All employment contracts must include the following security-related clauses:

### 4.1 Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

**Clause:**
> "The Employee acknowledges that during employment, they will have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to Union Eyes, its clients, and union members. The Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Maintain strict confidentiality of all such information during and after employment
> - Not disclose confidential information to any third party without prior written authorization
> - Not use confidential information for personal benefit or the benefit of others
> - Return all confidential information and materials upon termination of employment
> 
> This obligation survives termination of employment indefinitely for trade secrets and for five (5) years for other confidential information."

**Key Elements:**
- Broad definition of confidential information
- Prohibition on unauthorized disclosure and use
- Survival clause extending beyond employment termination
- Return of materials obligation

### 4.2 Information Security Responsibilities

**Clause:**
> "The Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Comply with all Union Eyes information security policies, standards, and procedures
> - Complete all mandatory security awareness training within timelines specified
> - Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication on all accounts
> - Protect login credentials and never share passwords with others
> - Report security incidents, suspected breaches, or vulnerabilities immediately
> - Use Union Eyes information systems only for authorized business purposes
> - Not install unauthorized software or circumvent security controls
> - Protect Union Eyes devices and data when working remotely
> 
> The Employee acknowledges that security policies may be updated from time to time and agrees to comply with updated policies upon notice."

**Key Elements:**
- Explicit obligation to follow security policies
- Training requirements
- Password and access control responsibilities
- Incident reporting duty
- Acceptable use principles
- Flexibility for policy updates

### 4.3 Acceptable Use of IT Systems

**Clause:**
> "Union Eyes IT systems, including computers, networks, email, internet access, and applications, are provided for business purposes. The Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Use IT systems primarily for legitimate business purposes
> - Not access, download, store, or transmit illegal, offensive, or inappropriate material
> - Not use systems in a manner that violates laws or infringes others' rights
> - Not attempt to gain unauthorized access to systems or data
> - Consent to monitoring of IT systems use as permitted by law
> - Not use company resources for personal business activities or competing ventures
> 
> Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with job performance, consume excessive resources, or violate any policy."

**Key Elements:**
- Prima facie business use requirement
- Prohibition on illegal and inappropriate use
- Monitoring consent
- Limited personal use allowance
- Prohibition on competitive activities

### 4.4 Data Protection and Privacy Obligations

**Clause:**
> "The Employee will have access to personal information of union members and other individuals. The Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Process personal information only as authorized and necessary for job duties
> - Comply with PIPEDA, GDPR, and all applicable privacy legislation
> - Implement appropriate safeguards to protect personal information
> - Not access personal information out of curiosity or for personal purposes
> - Report any privacy breaches or unauthorized access immediately
> - Complete mandatory privacy training as required
> 
> The Employee acknowledges that unauthorized access to or disclosure of personal information is a serious violation that may result in disciplinary action, including termination, and may expose the Employee to legal liability."

**Key Elements:**
- Privacy compliance obligation
- Purpose limitation principle
- Prohibition on unauthorized access
- Training requirement
- Accountability and consequences

### 4.5 Intellectual Property Assignment

**Clause:**
> "All work product, inventions, discoveries, software code, documentation, and creative works developed by the Employee during employment and relating to Union Eyes business or using Union Eyes resources are the exclusive property of Union Eyes. The Employee:
> 
> - Assigns all right, title, and interest in such work product to Union Eyes
> - Agrees to execute any documents necessary to perfect Union Eyes' ownership
> - Will not incorporate third-party intellectual property without authorization
> - Discloses any prior inventions or works to avoid assignment conflicts
> 
> This assignment includes, but is not limited to, copyrights, patents, trade secrets, and trademarks."

**Key Elements:**
- Broad work-for-hire assignment
- Cooperation in perfecting ownership
- Third-party IP protection
- Prior inventions disclosure

### 4.6 Conflicts of Interest and Outside Activities

**Clause:**
> "The Employee must avoid conflicts of interest and activities that compete with Union Eyes. The Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Not engage in competing business activities during employment
> - Not accept employment or consulting engagements with competitors
> - Disclose any outside business activities, board positions, or significant financial interests
> - Not use Union Eyes confidential information or business relationships for personal gain
> - Seek prior written approval for outside employment or business ventures
> 
> The Employee has a continuing duty to disclose potential conflicts of interest."

**Key Elements:**
- Non-compete during employment
- Disclosure obligations
- Prior approval requirement
- Duty to update

### 4.7 Remote Work and Security

**Clause (for remote workers):**
> "The Employee is authorized to work remotely and agrees to:
> 
> - Maintain a secure home office environment free from unauthorized access
> - Use only Union Eyes-approved devices for accessing company systems and data
> - Connect to Union Eyes systems only via secure, encrypted VPN connections
> - Protect Union Eyes devices from theft, loss, or unauthorized access
> - Not allow family members or others to use Union Eyes devices
> - Ensure home network is secured with strong passwords and encryption
> - Comply with all requirements in the Remote Work Security Policy
> - Report any loss, theft, or security incident involving Union Eyes devices immediately
> 
> Union Eyes reserves the right to revoke remote work privileges for security non-compliance."

**Key Elements:**
- Physical security of home office
- Device and network security
- VPN usage requirement
- Prohibition on sharing devices
- Right to revoke privileges

### 4.8 Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Terms

**Clause (if BYOD permitted):**
> "If authorized to use personal devices for work purposes, the Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Install and maintain Union Eyes Mobile Device Management (MDM) software
> - Comply with minimum security requirements (OS updates, screen lock, encryption)
> - Allow Union Eyes to remotely wipe corporate data from device if lost/stolen or upon termination
> - Not store sensitive personal information of union members on personal devices
> - Consent to limited monitoring of work-related data on the device
> - Understand that personal device use for work is a privilege that may be revoked
> 
> Union Eyes is not responsible for any personal data loss resulting from remote wipe or security measures."

**Key Elements:**
- MDM consent and requirements
- Remote wipe authorization
- Security baseline requirements
- Limitation on sensitive data
- Liability limitation

### 4.9 Security Incident Reporting

**Clause:**
> "The Employee has a mandatory duty to immediately report security incidents, including:
> 
> - Suspected or actual data breaches or unauthorized data access
> - Lost or stolen devices containing Union Eyes data
> - Phishing attempts or suspected malware infections
> - Unauthorized access attempts or suspicious system activity
> - Violations of security policy by the Employee or others
> - Vulnerabilities in systems or applications
> 
> Reports must be made to the Employee's manager and the security team (security@unioneyes.com) as soon as practicable, but no later than 4 hours after discovery.
> 
> The Employee will not be penalized for good-faith reporting of security incidents, including self-reporting of accidental policy violations."

**Key Elements:**
- Broad reporting obligation
- Examples of reportable incidents
- Reporting channel and timeline
- Non-retaliation assurance

### 4.10 Consequences of Security Violations

**Clause:**
> "Violations of Union Eyes security policies, procedures, or these terms and conditions may result in disciplinary action, up to and including:
> 
> - Verbal or written warning
> - Suspension of access privileges
> - Mandatory additional training
> - Suspension without pay
> - Termination of employment with cause
> 
> Serious violations involving theft, fraud, intentional data breaches, or gross negligence will result in immediate termination and may be referred to law enforcement for criminal prosecution. The Employee may also be held personally liable for damages resulting from security violations.
> 
> Union Eyes reserves the right to pursue all available legal remedies, including injunctive relief and damages, for breaches of these obligations."

**Key Elements:**
- Progressive discipline framework
- Immediate termination for serious violations
- Criminal referral possibility
- Personal liability
- Injunctive relief and damages

### 4.11 Monitoring and Audit Rights

**Clause:**
> "To protect information security and ensure policy compliance, Union Eyes reserves the right to:
> 
> - Monitor use of IT systems, including email, internet, and application usage
> - Audit files stored on company devices and systems
> - Review access logs and security event data
> - Inspect company-issued devices and premises
> - Conduct security audits and investigations
> 
> The Employee has no expectation of privacy in Union Eyes IT systems and consents to such monitoring as permitted by applicable law. Union Eyes will conduct monitoring in accordance with privacy legislation and will use information obtained only for legitimate business purposes."

**Key Elements:**
- Broad monitoring rights
- No expectation of privacy
- Audit and investigation rights
- Privacy law compliance commitment

### 4.12 Post-Employment Obligations

**Clause:**
> "Upon termination of employment for any reason, the Employee agrees to:
> 
> - Immediately return all Union Eyes property, including devices, access cards, keys, and documents
> - Permanently delete all Union Eyes data from personal devices
> - Cease all use of Union Eyes credentials, systems, and resources
> - Continue to maintain confidentiality of confidential information as required
> - Not solicit Union Eyes employees or clients for [12] months following termination
> - Cooperate with offboarding procedures and exit interview processes
> - Not disparage Union Eyes, its products, or personnel
> 
> These post-employment obligations are in addition to any non-disclosure, non-compete, or non-solicitation agreements signed separately."

**Key Elements:**
- Return of property
- Data deletion
- Access cessation
- Ongoing confidentiality
- Non-solicitation (jurisdictionally appropriate)
- Non-disparagement
- Cooperation with offboarding

## 5. Contractor and Third-Party Agreement Terms

### 5.1 Master Services Agreement (MSA) Terms

Agreements with contractors and consulting firms must include:

**Security Requirements Clause:**
> "The Contractor agrees to maintain security controls commensurate with the sensitivity of Union Eyes data and systems accessed. The Contractor will:
> 
> - Implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Union Eyes information
> - Comply with all Union Eyes security policies provided or made available
> - Use Union Eyes data only for the purposes specified in the Statement of Work
> - Not subcontract work involving Union Eyes data without prior written consent
> - Submit to security audits and provide evidence of compliance upon request
> - Maintain cyber insurance with minimum coverage of $2,000,000 (for significant engagements)
> - Notify Union Eyes of security incidents involving Union Eyes data within 4 hours
> - Return or destroy all Union Eyes data upon contract completion or termination"

**Key Elements:**
- Security control requirements
- Policy compliance obligation
- Purpose limitation
- Subcontracting restrictions
- Insurance requirements (scaled to engagement value)
- Incident notification
- Data return/destruction

### 5.2 Individual Contractor Agreements

Independent contractors must sign agreements including:
- All clauses in Section 4.1 through 4.12 (adapted for contractor context)
- Explicit statement that contractor is not an employee
- Indemnification clause for security breaches caused by contractor
- Right to audit contractor's security practices

## 6. Security Roles and Responsibilities

Employment agreements must specify security responsibilities for certain roles:

### 6.1 All Employees
- Protect confidential information
- Follow security policies
- Complete training
- Report incidents

### 6.2 Developers and Engineers
- Write secure code following secure coding standards
- Conduct peer code reviews with security focus
- Never commit secrets or credentials to source code repositories
- Participate in secure development lifecycle activities

### 6.3 Database Administrators and Infrastructure Engineers
- Implement least privilege access controls
- Maintain audit logs and monitoring
- Apply security patches promptly
- Follow change management procedures
- Protect administrative credentials

### 6.4 Managers and Team Leads
- Ensure direct reports complete training
- Enforce security policies within team
- Conduct security discussions in team meetings
- Report team members' security concerns to security team
- Participate in access reviews for team members

### 6.5 Security Team Members
- Act as security champions and role models
- Stay current on security threats and best practices
- Conduct security assessments and audits
- Respond to security incidents professionally
- Maintain confidentiality of vulnerability information

### 6.6 Executive Team
- Set tone at the top for security culture
- Allocate adequate resources to security
- Ensure organization-wide compliance with security policies
- Review security metrics and incident reports
- Accountability for security governance

## 7. Security Training Acknowledgment

All employment contracts must include:

**Training Acknowledgment Clause:**
> "The Employee acknowledges receiving access to Union Eyes security policies and agrees to:
> 
> - Review the Information Security Policy and related policies within 5 business days of hire
> - Complete mandatory security awareness training within 30 days of hire
> - Complete annual security refresher training
> - Complete role-specific security training as assigned
> - Acknowledge receipt and understanding of policy updates when provided
> 
> The Employee confirms understanding that failure to complete required training may result in suspension of system access until training is completed."

## 8. Agreement Administration

### 8.1 Document Creation and Review
- HR uses approved templates for all employment and contractor agreements
- Legal counsel reviews templates annually and upon material changes
- Security team reviews security clauses annually
- Hiring managers may not modify security clauses without approval

### 8.2 Signature and Retention
- All agreements signed before first day of work or system access
- Electronic signatures permitted using approved platforms (DocuSign, Adobe Sign)
- Signed agreements retained for duration of employment + 7 years
- Agreements stored securely with access limited to HR and authorized personnel

### 8.3 Amendments and Acknowledgments
- Material changes to security policies require written acknowledgment by employees
- Acknowledgment obtained within 30 days of policy update
- Acknowledgment documented and retained
- Failure to acknowledge may result in access suspension

### 8.4 Multi-Jurisdictional Considerations
- Agreements tailored to comply with employment law in employee's jurisdiction
- Non-compete and non-solicitation clauses only included where enforceable
- Monitoring and privacy clauses adapted to local privacy laws
- Legal counsel consulted for non-Canadian employees

## 9. Special Circumstances

### 9.1 Probationary Employees
- All security terms apply during probationary period
- May have additional monitoring during probation
- Enhanced supervision and access limitations
- Security performance evaluated as part of probation review

### 9.2 Interns and Co-op Students
- Simplified agreements but same core security obligations
- Enhanced supervision requirements
- Limited access to production systems
- Shorter post-employment confidentiality period (3 years)

### 9.3 Board Members and Advisors
- Separate confidentiality and security agreement
- Access limited to strategic information, typically not operational systems
- Same data protection and privacy obligations
- Annual re-acknowledgment of obligations

### 9.4 Executives with Equity
- Enhanced fiduciary duties and loyalty obligations
- Explicit acknowledgment of access to highly confidential strategic information
- Longer post-employment confidentiality period (unlimited for trade secrets)
- Board-level approval for certain outside activities

## 10. Enforcement and Consequences

### 10.1 Violation Response Process
1. **Detection:** Violation identified through monitoring, audit, or report
2. **Investigation:** Security team and HR investigate facts
3. **Assessment:** Severity assessed (minor, moderate, serious, critical)
4. **Disciplinary Action:** Appropriate response determined based on severity
5. **Documentation:** All steps documented in personnel file
6. **Notification:** Employee notified in writing
7. **Remediation:** Additional training or monitoring implemented

### 10.2 Disciplinary Matrix

| Severity | Examples | Typical Response |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| **Minor** | Accidental policy violation, late training completion | Verbal counseling, remedial training |
| **Moderate** | Repeated minor violations, password sharing, working without VPN | Written warning, mandatory training, monitoring |
| **Serious** | Unauthorized data access, circumventing security controls, egregious policy violation | Final written warning, suspension, or termination |
| **Critical** | Data theft, intentional breach, fraud, sabotage | Immediate termination, law enforcement referral, legal action |

### 10.3 Immediate Termination Offenses
- Theft or misappropriation of confidential information
- Intentional data breach or sabotage
- Fraud or embezzlement
- Hacking or unauthorized access with malicious intent
- Sharing credentials to facilitate unauthorized access by others
- Using Union Eyes data for personal financial gain
- Ignoring direct order to cease security violation

### 10.4 Legal Remedies
Union Eyes may pursue:
- Injunctive relief to prevent ongoing harm
- Compensatory damages for losses incurred
- Exemplary (punitive) damages for intentional breaches
- Costs of investigation and legal fees
- Reporting to law enforcement for criminal violations

## 11. Employee Rights and Protections

### 11.1 Good Faith Reporting Protection
- No retaliation for good-faith incident reporting
- Self-reporting of accidental violations encouraged
- Whistleblower protections for reporting others' violations
- Protected disclosure procedures available

### 11.2 Due Process
- Right to be informed of allegations
- Opportunity to provide explanation before discipline
- Right to review evidence (subject to confidentiality)
- Right to union representation where applicable
- Right to appeal disciplinary decisions

### 11.3 Privacy Rights
- Monitoring conducted in accordance with privacy law
- Personal use of systems permitted within reasonable limits
- Employee access to own monitoring data upon request
- Monitoring data used only for legitimate business purposes

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities

### 12.1 Human Resources Manager
- Ensure all employment agreements include required security clauses
- Obtain signatures and maintain agreement repository
- Coordinate acknowledgment of policy updates
- Support investigations of security violations
- Implement disciplinary actions in coordination with managers

### 12.2 Chief Security Officer (CSO)
- Define required security clauses and approve templates
- Review and approve exceptions to standard terms
- Participate in investigations of serious security violations
- Recommend disciplinary actions for security breaches
- Report on agreement compliance to executive team

### 12.3 Legal Counsel
- Draft and review employment agreement templates
- Ensure compliance with employment and privacy law
- Advise on multi-jurisdictional considerations
- Support enforcement actions and legal remedies
- Review and approve amendments to security clauses

### 12.4 Hiring Managers
- Communicate security expectations to candidates
- Ensure candidates understand and agree to security terms before hire
- Enforce security policies within team
- Initiate violation investigations and disciplinary process

### 12.5 Employees
- Read and understand employment agreement terms
- Comply with all security terms and conditions
- Ask questions if terms are unclear
- Acknowledge policy updates promptly
- Report violations by self or others

## 13. Metrics and Monitoring

### 13.1 Compliance Metrics
- Percentage of employees with signed agreements including security clauses: 100% target
- Average time to obtain signed agreement: <3 days target
- Percentage of employees completing policy acknowledgments within 30 days: 100% target
- Number of security violations by type and severity: tracked quarterly

### 13.2 Effectiveness Metrics
- Number of self-reported violations (indicator of reporting culture)
- Time to detect and respond to violations
- Recidivism rate (repeat violations by same employee)
- Security violation rate per headcount

### 13.3 Reporting
- Quarterly report to executive team: violations, disciplinary actions, trends
- Annual report to board: program effectiveness, systemic issues, recommendations

## 14. Policy Review and Updates

- Policy reviewed annually by HR, Security, and Legal
- Agreement templates updated to reflect policy changes
- Existing employees provided with updated terms for acknowledgment
- Material changes effective upon acknowledgment (or 30 days if no response)
- Updates communicated clearly with explanation of changes
