# Employee Screening Policy

**Policy Owner:** Chief Security Officer / HR Manager  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 12, 2026  
**Review Frequency:** Annual  
**ISO 27001:2022 Control:** A.6.1 - Screening

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the screening requirements for all employees, contractors, and third-party personnel at Union Eyes to ensure that individuals with access to sensitive union member data, financial information, and critical systems are trustworthy and meet the organization's security standards.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All prospective full-time and part-time employees
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Interns and co-op students
- Third-party personnel requiring regular access to Union Eyes systems or data
- Founders and executives (for external verification purposes)

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:
- ISO 27001:2022 (A.6.1 - Screening)
- SOC 2 Type II (Trust Services Criteria)
- PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- Provincial privacy legislation (where applicable)

## 4. Risk-Based Screening Levels

Union Eyes applies screening based on the level of access to sensitive information and critical systems:

### 4.1 Level 1 - Standard Screening
**Applies to:** Administrative staff, junior support roles with no direct database access

**Requirements:**
- Employment history verification (last 5 years)
- Two professional references
- Identity verification (government-issued ID)
- Right to work verification
- Criminal record check (vulnerable sector if accessing member data)

### 4.2 Level 2 - Enhanced Screening
**Applies to:** Software developers, database administrators, senior support staff, operations personnel

**Requirements:**
- All Level 1 checks
- Enhanced criminal record check (vulnerable sector screening)
- Education credential verification
- Professional certification verification (if applicable)
- Credit check (if handling financial data or payment systems)
- Social media review (public profiles only, for reputational risk)

### 4.3 Level 3 - Executive Screening  
**Applies to:** C-suite executives, security team, access administrators, financial controllers

**Requirements:**
- All Level 2 checks
- Enhanced criminal record check with five-year history
- Comprehensive employment history verification (10 years)
- Three professional references including previous supervisor
- Financial background check
- Regulatory violation check (if applicable)
- Conflict of interest declaration

## 5. Screening Components

### 5.1 Identity Verification
- Two pieces of government-issued identification required
- One must include a photograph (passport, driver's license)
- Verification of legal name, date of birth, and address
- Documentation retained securely in personnel file

### 5.2 Criminal Record Check
- **Canadian Residents:** RCMP-certified criminal record check or provincial equivalent
- **International Contractors:** International criminal record check or statutory declaration
- **Vulnerable Sector Screening:** Required for all roles with access to union member PII
- **Validity:** Must be dated within 6 months of hire date
- **Reassessment:** Every 5 years for ongoing employees in sensitive roles

**Disqualifying Offenses:**
- Fraud, theft, embezzlement, or financial crimes
- Identity theft or cybercrime offenses
- Convictions related to dishonesty or breach of trust
- Violent crimes or sexual offenses (absolute disqualification)

**Review Process:**
- All criminal records reviewed by HR Manager and CSO
- Case-by-case assessment considering nature, time elapsed, rehabilitation
- Documentation of decision rationale retained

### 5.3 Employment History Verification
- Verification of employment dates, job titles, and responsibilities
- Minimum last 5 years (10 years for executive roles)
- Gaps in employment exceeding 6 months must be explained
- Direct contact with previous employers where possible
- Written verification retained in personnel file

### 5.4 Reference Checks
- **Standard Roles:** Minimum 2 professional references
- **Enhanced Roles:** Minimum 2 professional references including one previous direct supervisor
- **Executive Roles:** Minimum 3 professional references including previous supervisor

**Reference Check Questions:**
- Employment dates and job title confirmation
- Quality of work and reliability
- Integrity and trustworthiness
- Reason for leaving
- Eligibility for rehire
- Any concerns regarding confidentiality or security

### 5.5 Education and Credential Verification
- Verification of highest degree claimed (for roles requiring specific education)
- Professional certifications verified directly with issuing body
- Required for: Software engineers, security professionals, financial roles
- Copy of diploma/certificate and verification documentation retained

### 5.6 Credit Check
**Required for:**
- Roles with access to financial systems or payment processing
- Financial controllers, accountants, treasury functions
- Senior executives with budget authority

**Process:**
- Candidate consent required (separate written authorization)
- Conducted by reputable credit bureau
- Focus on patterns indicating financial distress: bankruptcies, judgments, serious delinquencies
- Results reviewed confidentially by HR and CSO
- Not an automatic disqualification; assessed in context

### 5.7 Social Media and Online Presence Review
**Scope:** Publicly available information only (no friend requests or covert access)

**Purpose:**
- Identify reputational risks
- Detect publicly disclosed conflicts of interest
- Identify evidence of discriminatory behavior or harassment
- Verify professional claims and affiliations

**Limitations:**
- No consideration of protected characteristics (race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.)
- Documented rationale for any concerns raised
- Review conducted by trained personnel to avoid bias

## 6. International Contractors and Remote Workers

### 6.1 Challenges
Remote-first organizations face unique challenges verifying international personnel.

### 6.2 Requirements
- National criminal record check from country of residence (or statutory declaration if unavailable)
- International reference checks (may use video calls)
- Identity verification via video call with original documents shown
- Use of third-party international screening services where available
- Additional probationary monitoring period (90 days)

### 6.3 Approved Screening Vendors
- Maintain list of approved third-party screening providers for international checks
- Vendors must comply with local privacy laws and GDPR where applicable

## 7. Consent and Privacy

### 7.1 Candidate Consent
- Written consent obtained before initiating any background checks
- Consent form explains:
  - Types of checks to be conducted
  - How information will be used
  - Who will have access to results
  - Retention period
  - Candidate's rights

### 7.2 Privacy and Data Protection
- Screening data processed lawfully under PIPEDA and GDPR
- Screening results stored securely with access limited to HR and CSO
- Results retained for duration of employment plus 7 years (or as required by law)
- Candidate has right to access and correct information
- Third-party screening vendors must sign Data Processing Agreement

### 7.3 Adverse Action Process
If screening reveals disqualifying information:
1. Candidate notified in writing of adverse finding
2. Copy of screening report provided to candidate
3. Opportunity provided to dispute or explain (5 business days)
4. Final decision documented with rationale
5. Screening records retained per retention schedule

## 8. Screening Timeline

- **Job Offer:** Conditional offer contingent on satisfactory screening
- **Initiation:** Screening initiated within 2 business days of conditional offer acceptance
- **Completion Target:** 10-15 business days (may vary for international checks)
- **Provisional Access:** No access to production systems or sensitive data until screening complete
- **Start Date:** May be delayed if screening incomplete

## 9. Ongoing and Periodic Re-Screening

### 9.1 Trigger-Based Re-Screening
Re-screening required upon:
- Promotion to role with elevated access (apply new screening level)
- Transfer to role handling financial transactions
- Security incident involving the employee
- Return from extended leave (>6 months)

### 9.2 Periodic Re-Screening
- **High-Risk Roles (Level 3):** Criminal record check every 5 years
- **Financial Roles:** Credit check every 3 years
- **All Employees:** Self-declaration of criminal charges annually

## 10. Screening for Internal Promotions

- Employees promoted to higher-risk roles undergo incremental screening
- Example: Developer promoted to DBA undergoes enhanced criminal record check if not previously completed
- Existing employment history and references may be used if recent

## 11. Contractor and Third-Party Screening

### 11.1 Individual Contractors
- Subject to same screening as employees based on access level
- Screening conducted before contract execution
- Renewal contracts >6 months after previous screening require updated checks

### 11.2 Third-Party Companies
- Written attestation that company conducts equivalent screening for assigned personnel
- Right to request proof of screening for individuals with access to Union Eyes data
- Third-party company must notify Union Eyes of any security-relevant changes in assigned personnel

## 12. Record Retention

- **Screening Results:** Duration of employment + 7 years
- **Unsuccessful Candidates:** 1 year from screening completion
- **Criminal Record Checks:** Secure storage; access restricted to HR and CSO
- **Consent Forms:** Duration of employment + 7 years

## 13. Roles and Responsibilities

### 13.1 Human Resources Manager
- Oversee screening process for all candidates
- Obtain candidate consent and initiate background checks
- Coordinate with third-party screening vendors
- Maintain screening records securely
- Ensure compliance with privacy legislation

### 13.2 Chief Security Officer (CSO)
- Determine screening level requirements for each role
- Review criminal record checks and security-relevant findings
- Make final determination on security clearance
- Conduct periodic re-screening risk assessments
- Approve exceptions to this policy

### 13.3 Hiring Manager
- Communicate screening requirements to candidates
- Delay provisioning access until screening complete
- Report any security concerns about employees to HR and CSO

### 13.4 Employees and Contractors
- Provide accurate information on employment applications
- Consent to required background checks
- Disclose any criminal charges or convictions during employment
- Report changes in circumstances affecting security clearance

## 14. Exceptions and Special Cases

### 14.1 Exception Process
- Exceptions to screening requirements require written approval from CSO and CEO
- Rationale documented and retained
- Compensating controls implemented (e.g., restricted access, increased monitoring)

### 14.2 Emergency Hires
- Critical roles requiring immediate fill may receive provisional access
- Provisional access limited: no production data access, supervised work only
- Screening expedited with results within 5 business days
- Access revoked if screening not completed within 10 business days

### 14.3 Founder and Executive Self-Screening
- Founders conduct mutual screening or engage independent third party
- Demonstrates commitment to security culture
- Documented for auditor review

## 15. Training and Awareness

- HR personnel trained on screening procedures, privacy requirements, and bias avoidance
- Hiring managers briefed on screening policy and timelines
- Annual review of screening procedures by HR and CSO

## 16. Policy Compliance and Monitoring

### 16.1 Compliance Checks
- HR maintains screening completion tracker for all personnel
- Quarterly audit: 100% screening completion for all active employees
- Annual review of screening policy effectiveness

### 16.2 Metrics and Reporting
- Time-to-complete screening (target: 10 business days)
- Percentage of employees with current screening (target: 100%)
- Number of adverse findings and disqualifications
- Reported quarterly to executive team

### 16.3 Audit Trail
- All screening activities logged: initiation date, completion date, reviewer, outcome
- Audit trail available for internal and external audits

## 17. Policy Review and Updates

- Policy reviewed annually by HR Manager and CSO
- Updated as needed to reflect:
  - Changes in regulatory requirements
  - Changes in risk profile or business operations
  - Lessons learned from security incidents
  - Audit or assessment recommendations
