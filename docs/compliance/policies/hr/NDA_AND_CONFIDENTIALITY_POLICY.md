# NDA and Confidentiality Policy

**Policy Owner:** Chief Security Officer / Legal Counsel  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 12, 2026  
**Review Frequency:** Annual  
**ISO 27001:2022 Control:** A.6.6 - Confidentiality or Non-Disclosure Agreements

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and confidentiality obligations at Union Eyes. It ensures that all personnel, contractors, partners, and third parties with access to confidential information understand their obligations to protect Union Eyes' confidential information, trade secrets, and sensitive data including union member personal information.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Independent contractors and consultants
- Board members and advisors
- Vendors and service providers with access to confidential information
- Business partners and strategic alliance partners
- Potential investors, acquirers, or partners (under evaluation)
- Interns and co-op students
- Any third party receiving Union Eyes confidential information

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:
- ISO 27001:2022 (A.6.6 - Confidentiality or Non-Disclosure Agreements)
- SOC 2 Type II (Confidentiality Criteria)
- PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- Canadian common law and provincial trade secrets legislation
- U.S. Defend Trade Secrets Act (for U.S. operations, if applicable)

## 4. Types of Confidential Information

### 4.1 Definition of Confidential Information

**Confidential Information** includes all information, whether written, oral, electronic, or visual, that is:
- Not publicly available
- Identified as confidential or proprietary
- Reasonably understood to be confidential given its nature or context

### 4.2 Categories of Confidential Information

#### Business and Strategic Information
- Business plans, strategies, and forecasts
- Financial information, budgets, and projections
- Marketing plans and customer analytics
- Pricing information and fee structures
- Revenue and sales data
- Merger, acquisition, or partnership negotiations
- Intellectual property and product roadmaps
- Competitive analyses and market research

#### Technical Information
- Source code and software architecture
- Algorithms and technical specifications
- Database schemas and data models
- API documentation and integration specifications
- Infrastructure configurations and network diagrams
- Security controls and vulnerability assessments
- Technical documentation and runbooks
- Development methodologies and tooling

#### Customer and Union Member Information
- **Union member PII:** Names, addresses, contact information, social insurance numbers
- **Financial data:** Bank account details, payment information, transaction history
- **Membership data:** Union affiliations, dues, voting history, grievances
- **Employment information:** Employer details, employment history, wage information
- **Union leadership information:** Officers, delegates, representative contact details
- **Contract and CBA information:** Collective bargaining agreements, negotiation details
- **Analytics and insights:** Member behavior, engagement metrics, predictive analytics

#### Vendor and Partner Information
- Vendor contracts and pricing
- Service provider access credentials
- Partner business terms and agreements
- Third-party technical integrations
- Joint development or co-marketing plans

#### Internal Operations
- Employee personal information and HR records
- Compensation structures and salary bands
- Internal policies and procedures (marked confidential)
- Security incident details and investigations
- Audit reports and compliance assessments
- Legal matters and attorney communications

### 4.3 Trade Secrets

**Trade Secrets** are a subset of confidential information entitled to stronger protection:
- Information that derives economic value from not being generally known
- Subject to reasonable efforts to maintain secrecy
- Examples: proprietary algorithms, customer lists, pricing models, technical know-how

**Special protection:**
- Indefinite confidentiality period (no expiration)
- Enhanced security measures
- Limited disclosure even internally
- Specific identification in agreements

### 4.4 Exclusions from Confidential Information

Information is **NOT** confidential if:
- Publicly available through no breach of confidentiality obligation
- Independently developed by receiving party without access to confidential information
- Rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality restriction
- Required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (with proper notice to disclosing party)
- Explicitly identified as non-confidential in writing by disclosing party

## 5. NDA Requirements by Relationship

### 5.1 Employees

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory for all employees

**Timing:** Signed before first day of employment or access to confidential information

**Form:** 
- Standalone NDA, or
- Confidentiality clause in employment agreement (see [Employment Terms and Security Policy](EMPLOYMENT_TERMS_SECURITY_POLICY.md))

**Duration:**
- **During employment:** All confidential information protected
- **Post-employment:** 
  - Trade secrets: Indefinite protection
  - Other confidential information: 5 years post-termination
  - Personal data of union members: Indefinite protection under privacy law

**Key Terms:**
- Broad definition of confidential information
- Prohibition on unauthorized disclosure and use
- Return of materials upon termination
- Acknowledgment of damages for breach
- Survival of obligations beyond employment

**Template:** Employee NDA Template (Appendix A)

### 5.2 Independent Contractors and Consultants

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory before engagement

**Timing:** Signed before contract execution or access to any confidential information

**Form:** 
- Standalone mutual NDA (if contractor also sharing confidential information), or
- Unilateral NDA (Union Eyes disclosing to contractor), or
- Confidentiality clause in Master Services Agreement or Statement of Work

**Duration:**
- **During engagement:** All confidential information protected
- **Post-engagement:** 
  - Trade secrets: Indefinite
  - Other confidential information: 5 years post-contract
  - Personal data: Indefinite under privacy law

**Key Terms:**
- All employee NDA terms, plus:
- Prohibition on subcontracting without consent
- Contractor indemnification for breaches
- Survival of obligations beyond contract term
- Right to audit contractor's compliance

**Template:** Contractor NDA Template (Appendix B)

### 5.3 Vendors and Service Providers

**NDA Requirement:** Required if vendor has access to:
- Union Eyes confidential business information
- Source code or technical documentation
- Union member PII or sensitive data
- Financial information
- Internal systems or infrastructure

**Timing:** Before granting access to confidential information

**Form:**
- Mutual NDA (typical for vendor relationships), or
- Confidentiality provisions in Master Services Agreement, or
- Vendor's standard NDA (reviewed and approved by Union Eyes Legal)

**Duration:**
- During contract term plus 3-5 years post-termination

**Key Terms:**
- Data protection and privacy compliance (PIPEDA, GDPR)
- Subcontractor restrictions and flow-down obligations
- Data breach notification requirements
- Return or destruction of data upon contract termination
- Liability and indemnification
- Insurance requirements

**Template:** Mutual NDA Template (Appendix C)

### 5.4 Business Partners and Strategic Alliances

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory for partnerships involving:
- Joint product development
- Co-marketing or reseller relationships
- Technology integrations
- Strategic initiatives
- Shared customer relationships

**Timing:** Before discussing partnership terms or sharing confidential information

**Form:** Mutual NDA (both parties typically disclose confidential information)

**Duration:** 3-5 years post-termination of partnership discussions or relationship

**Key Terms:**
- Mutual obligations (both parties bound)
- Purpose limitation: confidential information used only for partnership purposes
- Restrictions on use for competitive purposes
- Return of information if partnership doesn't proceed
- Permitted disclosures to advisors and representatives
- Publicity and announcement restrictions

**Template:** Mutual NDA Template (Appendix C)

### 5.5 Potential Investors, Acquirers, M&A Due Diligence

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory before sharing financial data, customer information, or strategic plans

**Timing:** Before providing access to data room or confidential materials

**Form:** Unilateral NDA (Union Eyes disclosing to investor/buyer)

**Duration:** 2-3 years (shorter for term sheets; longer for due diligence materials)

**Key Terms:**
- Prohibition on use of information for trading or competitive purposes
- Standstill provisions (no hostile acquisition attempts)
- Non-solicitation of employees
- Restrictions on contact with customers or partners
- Return or destruction of materials if transaction doesn't proceed
- Confidentiality of discussions themselves
- No obligation to proceed with transaction

**Template:** Investor/M&A NDA Template (specific, consult legal)

### 5.6 Board Members and Advisors

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory before joining board or advisory role

**Timing:** Before first board meeting or advisory session

**Form:** Standalone NDA or confidentiality provisions in board/advisor agreement

**Duration:** Indefinite for trade secrets; 5 years for other information

**Key Terms:**
- Fiduciary duties (for board members)
- Broad confidentiality covering all board discussions and materials
- Prohibition on trading on material non-public information
- Conflicts of interest disclosure
- Return of materials upon resignation or removal

**Template:** Board/Advisor NDA Template (specific)

### 5.7 Interns and Co-op Students

**NDA Requirement:** Mandatory before access to confidential information

**Timing:** Before start date

**Form:** Simplified NDA or confidentiality clause in internship agreement

**Duration:** 
- During internship plus 3 years post-internship (shorter than employees given limited exposure)

**Key Terms:**
- Similar to employee NDA but simplified language
- Educational purpose and learning goals acknowledged
- Prohibition on including confidential information in academic work without permission
- Supervisor/mentor responsibilities

**Template:** Intern NDA Template (simplified, Appendix D)

## 6. NDA Templates and Standard Terms

### 6.1 Standard Contractual Clauses

All Union Eyes NDAs must include the following core terms:

#### 6.1.1 Definition of Confidential Information
Clear, broad definition covering all forms of information and including examples relevant to Union Eyes business.

#### 6.1.2 Obligations of Receiving Party
- Maintain confidentiality and not disclose to third parties
- Use confidential information only for authorized purposes
- Protect with at least same degree of care as own confidential information (but no less than reasonable care)
- Limit internal disclosure to need-to-know basis
- Ensure recipients are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations

#### 6.1.3 Exclusions (Standard Carve-Outs)
- Information already publicly available
- Information independently developed
- Information rightfully received from third party
- Information required to be disclosed by law (with notice)

#### 6.1.4 Term and Survival
- Agreement effective date and initial term
- Survival of confidentiality obligations beyond term
- Different durations for trade secrets vs. other information

#### 6.1.5 Return or Destruction of Materials
- Upon termination or request, all confidential materials returned or destroyed
- Certification of destruction if requested

#### 6.1.6 No License or Ownership Transfer
- Disclosure does not grant license or rights to confidential information
- Ownership remains with disclosing party

#### 6.1.7 Remedies and Enforcement
- Acknowledgment that breach may cause irreparable harm
- Equitable relief (injunction) available in addition to damages
- Prevailing party entitled to attorneys' fees (optional)

#### 6.1.8 No Warranty
- Confidential information provided "as is" without warranty
- No representation of accuracy or completeness (for third-party NDAs)

#### 6.1.9 Governing Law and Jurisdiction
- Ontario law (or jurisdiction appropriate to parties)
- Jurisdiction and venue for disputes

#### 6.1.10 Miscellaneous
- Entire agreement clause
- Amendment and waiver provisions
- Severability
- No implied waiver
- Counterparts and electronic signatures permitted

### 6.2 Union Eyes-Specific Required Terms

In addition to standard clauses, Union Eyes NDAs must include:

#### 6.2.1 Data Protection and Privacy Compliance
- Explicit acknowledgment that confidential information may include personal information subject to PIPEDA and GDPR
- Agreement to process personal information only as authorized and in compliance with privacy laws
- Prohibition on transfer of personal information outside Canada without consent (or to non-adequate jurisdictions under GDPR)
- Data breach notification obligation (within 24 hours)

#### 6.2.2 Security Controls
- Receiving party agrees to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect confidential information
- Measures commensurate with sensitivity of information
- Examples: encryption, access controls, secure storage

#### 6.2.3 Subcontractors and Representatives
- Disclosure to subcontractors or representatives only with prior written consent, or
- Permitted disclosure to employees, contractors, and advisors on need-to-know basis provided they are bound by equivalent obligations
- Receiving party liable for breaches by representatives

#### 6.2.4 No Reverse Engineering (for Technical NDAs)
- Prohibition on reverse engineering, decompiling, or disassembling software or technical materials

#### 6.2.5 Audit Rights (for Vendor/Partner NDAs)
- Union Eyes reserves right to audit receiving party's compliance with confidentiality obligations
- Reasonable notice and business hours
- Receiving party cooperation required

#### 6.2.6 Notification of Unauthorized Disclosure
- Receiving party must notify Union Eyes immediately upon discovery of any unauthorized disclosure or use
- Cooperation in investigation and remediation
- Assistance in mitigating harm

### 6.3 Mutual vs. Unilateral NDAs

**Unilateral NDA:**
- Union Eyes discloses confidential information to third party
- Third party has confidentiality obligations
- Union Eyes has no confidentiality obligations
- **Use for:** Employees, contractors, vendors (Union Eyes disclosing), investors/due diligence

**Mutual NDA:**
- Both parties disclose confidential information to each other
- Reciprocal confidentiality obligations
- Balanced terms
- **Use for:** Business partners, strategic alliances, potential customers, vendor evaluations (Union Eyes also receiving confidential info)

**Determining which to use:**
- If Union Eyes is only disclosing: Unilateral
- If both parties sharing information: Mutual
- If third party requests mutual but Union Eyes not sharing: Negotiate or decline

## 7. NDA Signature and Execution Requirements

### 7.1 Authority to Sign

**Union Eyes signatories:**
- CEO or CFO: Unlimited authority
- Legal Counsel: Unlimited authority for NDAs
- VP level or above: Authority for standard-form NDAs (up to $X million liability)
- Managers: No signature authority (must escalate)

**Third-party signatories:**
- Individual: Personal signature required
- Corporation: Authorized officer or representative
- Verification of authority for high-value or high-risk NDAs

### 7.2 Execution Process

**Before signing NDA:**
1. Determine appropriate NDA type (unilateral vs. mutual, template)
2. Use approved template or legal review if custom
3. Confirm signatory authority
4. Complete signature blocks with full names and titles
5. Execute via wet signature, electronic signature (DocuSign, Adobe Sign), or PDF exchange

**After signing NDA:**
1. Fully executed NDA stored in contract repository
2. Metadata recorded: parties, effective date, expiration date, type
3. Receiving party provided with fully executed copy
4. Compliance monitoring and calendar reminders for expiration

### 7.3 Electronic Signatures

- Electronic signatures legally binding in Canada (PIPEDA, provincial electronic commerce acts)
- Approved platforms: DocuSign, Adobe Sign, HelloSign
- Same legal effect as wet signatures
- Audit trail maintained by platform

### 7.4 Amendments and Waivers

- Any amendment to NDA must be in writing and signed by both parties
- Waiver of any provision must be in writing
- No implied waivers by conduct or delay

## 8. Acceptable Use of Confidential Information

### 8.1 Purpose Limitation

Confidential information may only be used for the purpose for which it was disclosed:
- **Employees:** Job duties and responsibilities
- **Contractors:** Services specified in Statement of Work
- **Vendors:** Providing contracted services
- **Partners:** Partnership purposes only
- **Investors:** Evaluation of investment opportunity

**Prohibited uses:**
- Competitive purposes
- Personal benefit or gain
- Disclosure to competitors
- Use for unrelated business purposes
- Incorporation into receiving party's products without license

### 8.2 Need-to-Know Principle

- Confidential information disclosed internally only to those who need to know
- Recipients must have legitimate business reason for access
- Recipients must be bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations (employment agreements, NDAs)
- Unnecessary disclosure increases risk of breach

### 8.3 Handling and Storage

**Physical confidential information:**
- Stored in locked cabinets or secure areas
- Not left unattended in open areas
- Shredded or destroyed securely when no longer needed

**Electronic confidential information:**
- Stored on encrypted systems or secure cloud storage
- Access controls and authentication required
- Not stored on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
- Not sent via unencrypted email (use secure file sharing)

**Transmission:**
- Confidential information transmitted securely (encrypted email, secure file transfer, VPN)
- Labeled or marked as "Confidential" when transmitted
- Verify recipient before sending

### 8.4 Marking and Labeling

**Recommended practice:**
- Mark documents, files, emails as "Confidential" or "Proprietary"
- NDA effectiveness not dependent on marking (Union Eyes policy: confidential even if not marked)
- Marking helps recipients recognize sensitivity and handle appropriately

**Marking methods:**
- Headers/footers: "Union Eyes Confidential"
- Watermarks on presentations or documents
- Email subject lines: [CONFIDENTIAL]
- File names or metadata

### 8.5 Conversations and Oral Disclosures

- Oral disclosures also covered by NDA
- Follow up in writing if practical: "As discussed, the following is confidential..."
- Avoid discussing confidential information in public places (cafes, public transit, airports)
- Use discretion in open offices or shared workspaces

## 9. Permissible Disclosures

### 9.1 Legally Compelled Disclosure

If recipient is required to disclose confidential information by law, regulation, or court order:

**Process:**
1. Provide Union Eyes with prompt written notice of request (before disclosure if possible)
2. Allow Union Eyes opportunity to seek protective order or contest disclosure
3. Cooperate with Union Eyes in limiting disclosure
4. Disclose only the minimum information required
5. Request confidential treatment of information disclosed

**Examples:**
- Subpoena or court order in litigation
- Request by regulatory authority (e.g., privacy commissioner, tax authority)
- Statutory reporting obligations

**Note:** Privacy legislation (PIPEDA, GDPR) permits disclosure of personal information for certain legal purposes.

### 9.2 Disclosure to Representatives

NDAs typically permit disclosure to:
- Employees on need-to-know basis
- Contractors and consultants assisting with authorized purpose (if bound by equivalent NDA)
- Legal and financial advisors (subject to professional confidentiality obligations)
- Auditors and accountants

**Conditions:**
- Receiving party remains liable for breaches by representatives
- Representatives must be informed of confidentiality obligations

### 9.3 Disclosure to Subcontractors

- Generally requires prior written consent from Union Eyes
- Subcontractor must sign flow-down NDA (same terms)
- Receiving party liable for subcontractor's breaches
- Union Eyes may audit subcontractor's compliance

## 10. Enforcement and Breach Response

### 10.1 Breach Indicators

Signs of potential NDA breach:
- Confidential information appearing in public domain (media, competitor websites, social media)
- Unauthorized disclosure by employee or contractor
- Former employee using confidential information at new employer
- Third party demonstrating knowledge they should not have
- Data breach or cybersecurity incident involving confidential data

### 10.2 Breach Investigation

**Upon suspected breach:**
1. Gather facts: who, what, when, where, how
2. Assess severity and scope of disclosure
3. Determine if NDA breach occurred
4. Identify responsible party
5. Evaluate harm and legal exposure
6. Engage legal counsel

**Evidence collection:**
- Emails, documents, or communications evidencing disclosure
- Witness statements
- Forensic analysis of data access or exfiltration
- Public domain sources showing disclosure

### 10.3 Breach Response and Remediation

**Immediate actions:**
1. Notify breaching party of suspected breach
2. Demand cessation of unauthorized use or disclosure
3. Request return or destruction of confidential information
4. Preserve evidence
5. Assess need for regulatory notification (if personal data breach)

**Legal remedies:**
- **Cease and desist letter:** Formal demand to stop breach
- **Injunctive relief:** Court order to stop disclosure or use (pre-trial or permanent)
- **Monetary damages:** Compensation for losses caused by breach
- **Exemplary (punitive) damages:** For willful or malicious breaches
- **Account of profits:** Breaching party disgorges profits from misuse
- **Attorneys' fees and costs:** If NDA provides for fee-shifting

**Criminal options (in limited cases):**
- Trade secret theft may constitute criminal offense under Canadian or U.S. law
- Report to law enforcement if theft or espionage suspected

### 10.4 Enforcement Decision Factors

**When deciding whether to enforce NDA:**
- Severity of breach (harmless vs. competitive damage)
- Nature of information disclosed (low vs. high sensitivity)
- Intent (inadvertent vs. willful)
- Harm caused or potential harm
- Relationship with breaching party (employee vs. partner vs. competitor)
- Likelihood of success in enforcement action
- Cost-benefit analysis of litigation

**Range of responses:**
- Internal discipline (employees)
- Warning letter or discussion (minor breaches)
- Termination of contract or relationship (vendors, contractors)
- Cease and desist letter
- Negotiated settlement
- Litigation (last resort)

## 11. Employee and Contractor Confidentiality Training

### 11.1 Training Requirements

**All personnel must receive training on:**
- Definition of confidential information
- NDA obligations and restrictions
- Proper handling and protection of confidential information
- What to do if confidential information is inadvertently disclosed
- Consequences of breaches

**Training delivery:**
- Onboarding security training (see [Security Awareness Training Policy](SECURITY_AWARENESS_TRAINING_POLICY.md))
- Annual refresher on confidentiality obligations
- Just-in-time reminders for high-risk scenarios (e.g., before conferences, partner meetings)

### 11.2 Awareness Campaigns

**Ongoing reminders:**
- Quarterly newsletter or email: confidentiality tips
- Security awareness posters highlighting confidentiality
- Scenario-based training: "What would you do?" confidentiality dilemmas
- Executive messaging reinforcing confidentiality culture

### 11.3 High-Risk Scenarios

**Special reminders for:**
- Attending industry conferences or trade shows (no inadvertent disclosure)
- Discussions with press or media (refer to PR/Communications)
- Social media use (no posting confidential information)
- Travel (protect devices and documents while traveling)
- Work in public spaces (no discussions or screen visibility)
- Interviews with prospective employees (share only what's necessary)

## 12. Third-Party NDA Management

### 12.1 NDA Repository and Tracking

**Contract management system:**
- All executed NDAs stored in centralized repository (e.g., DocuSign, contract management software, secure SharePoint)
- Metadata captured: parties, effective date, expiration date, type, status
- Search and retrieval functionality
- Expiration reminders and renewal workflows

**Responsible party:** Legal or HR (for employee NDAs) or Procurement (for vendor NDAs)

### 12.2 NDA Lifecycle Management

**Stages:**
1. **Initiation:** NDA need identified; template selected
2. **Negotiation:** Terms discussed and finalized (if custom)
3. **Execution:** Signed by authorized parties
4. **Storage:** Uploaded to repository
5. **Monitoring:** Compliance monitoring and expiration tracking
6. **Renewal or Expiration:** Renewed if relationship continues; archived if expired
7. **Enforcement:** Action taken if breach suspected

### 12.3 Renewal and Expiration

**Approaching expiration:**
- 60 days before expiration: Review whether NDA needs renewal
- If relationship continuing: Extend or execute new NDA
- If relationship ended: Allow expiration; obligations survive per survival clause

**Post-expiration:**
- Confidentiality obligations survive expiration per NDA terms (typically 3-5 years, indefinite for trade secrets)
- NDA archived but retained per retention policy
- No new disclosures after expiration

## 13. Exceptions and Escalations

### 13.1 Exception Requests

**Scenarios requiring approval:**
- Deviation from standard NDA templates
- Accepting third-party NDA with unfavorable terms
- Disclosure of highly sensitive information (trade secrets, member PII in bulk)
- Multi-party NDAs
- International parties with foreign law governing

**Approval process:**
1. Requestor identifies need for exception
2. Completes exception request form with rationale
3. Legal Counsel reviews and assesses risk
4. CSO approval required for security-sensitive disclosures
5. CEO approval for material business risk
6. Decision documented and filed with NDA

### 13.2 Escalation for NDA Breaches

**Escalation path:**
- Minor breach by employee: Manager and HR
- Serious breach by employee: HR, Legal, CSO, CEO
- Breach by third party: Legal Counsel leads response
- Criminal breach (espionage, theft): CEO, Legal, law enforcement

## 14. International Considerations

### 14.1 Cross-Border Data Transfers

**GDPR and PIPEDA restrictions:**
- Personal data transferred outside Canada/EU requires legal basis
- NDA alone insufficient; may require Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Consult legal counsel for international transfers of personal information

### 14.2 Multi-Jurisdictional NDAs

**Considerations:**
- Governing law and jurisdiction (prefer Ontario/Canadian law for Union Eyes)
- Enforceability of NDAs varies by jurisdiction
- Some jurisdictions may not recognize certain remedies (e.g., injunctive relief)
- Language requirements (e.g., Quebec - French language)
- Consult local counsel for non-Canadian parties in significant transactions

## 15. Roles and Responsibilities

### 15.1 Legal Counsel
- Draft and maintain NDA templates
- Review and approve custom or third-party NDAs
- Advise on enforceability and risk
- Lead enforcement actions for breaches
- Provide legal guidance on confidentiality matters
- Manage NDA repository (or delegate to appropriate team)

### 15.2 Chief Security Officer (CSO)
- Define confidential information and classification levels
- Approve disclosures of highly sensitive information
- Participate in breach investigations
- Ensure security controls align with confidentiality obligations
- Monitor compliance with handling requirements

### 15.3 Human Resources
- Ensure all employees sign NDAs before start date
- Manage employee NDA repository
- Enforce confidentiality obligations (discipline for breaches)
- Conduct confidentiality training
- Support exit interviews reminding of ongoing obligations

### 15.4 Procurement / Vendor Management
- Ensure vendors and contractors sign NDAs before access to confidential information
- Maintain vendor NDA repository
- Monitor compliance with vendor confidentiality obligations
- Coordinate with Legal on vendor NDA issues

### 15.5 Managers and Employees
- Identify when NDA is needed (before disclosing confidential information)
- Request NDA execution via appropriate channel
- Protect confidential information per policy and NDA
- Report suspected breaches
- Comply with all confidentiality obligations

## 16. Metrics and Monitoring

### 16.1 Compliance Metrics
- **NDA coverage:** % of employees, contractors, vendors with executed NDAs (target: 100%)
- **Execution timeliness:** % of NDAs executed before confidential disclosure (target: 100%)
- **Template usage:** % using approved templates vs. custom (target: >90% templates)
- **Training completion:** % of employees completing confidentiality training (target: 100%)

### 16.2 Effectiveness Metrics
- Number of NDA breaches detected (lower is better, but zero may indicate lack of monitoring)
- Time to detect breach (average days)
- Resolution rate for breach incidents
- Legal costs for enforcement actions
- Confidential information leaked or publicly disclosed (incidents)

### 16.3 Reporting
- Quarterly review of NDA compliance by Legal and CSO
- Annual report to board on confidentiality program effectiveness
- Breach incidents reported to executive team immediately

## 17. Policy Review and Updates

- Policy reviewed annually by Legal Counsel and CSO
- NDA templates reviewed annually and updated as needed
- Updates for:
  - Legal and regulatory changes
  - Case law or precedents impacting enforceability
  - Business changes (new types of relationships)
  - Lessons learned from breaches or enforcement actions
- All changes approved by CEO
