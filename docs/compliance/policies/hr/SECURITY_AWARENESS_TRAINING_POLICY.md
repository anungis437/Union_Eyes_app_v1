# Security Awareness Training Policy

**Policy Owner:** Chief Security Officer  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 12, 2026  
**Review Frequency:** Annual  
**ISO 27001:2022 Control:** A.6.3 - Information Security Awareness, Education and Training

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes Union Eyes' security awareness training program to ensure that all personnel understand their security responsibilities, recognize security threats, and know how to respond to security incidents. The program aims to create a security-conscious culture and reduce human-related security risks.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All full-time and part-time employees
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Interns and co-op students
- Board members and advisors
- Third-party personnel with access to Union Eyes systems or data
- Executives and founders

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

This policy supports compliance with:
- ISO 27001:2022 (A.6.3 - Information Security Awareness, Education and Training)
- SOC 2 Type II (Trust Services Criteria - Security)
- PIPEDA and GDPR (training on privacy obligations)
- PCI DSS (if applicable - security awareness for personnel handling payment data)

## 4. Training Program Overview

Union Eyes security awareness training program consists of:

1. **Onboarding Security Training** - Mandatory for all new hires (Day 1-30)
2. **Annual Security Refresher Training** - Mandatory for all personnel (yearly)
3. **Role-Based Security Training** - For personnel in specific security-sensitive roles
4. **Phishing Simulations** - Ongoing simulated phishing tests (monthly)
5. **Security Awareness Campaigns** - Regular security tips and awareness content
6. **Specialized Training** - Ad-hoc training for new threats or incidents
7. **Executive Security Briefings** - Quarterly briefings for executive team

## 5. Onboarding Security Training

### 5.1 Timing and Requirements
- **Must be completed:** Within 30 calendar days of hire date
- **System access:** Production access contingent on training completion
- **Duration:** Approximately 90 minutes
- **Delivery method:** Online self-paced modules + live orientation session
- **Completion tracking:** Learning Management System (LMS) with completion certificates

### 5.2 Onboarding Training Content

#### Module 1: Introduction to Information Security at Union Eyes (15 min)
- Overview of Union Eyes' security posture and culture
- Why security matters: sensitive union member data, financial information
- Regulatory compliance requirements (PIPEDA, GDPR, SOC 2)
- Security organization: security team, incident response contacts
- Employee security responsibilities

#### Module 2: Information Security Policies and Procedures (20 min)
- Information Security Policy overview
- Acceptable Use Policy key points
- Confidentiality and NDA obligations
- Data classification and handling requirements
- Physical security requirements (office and remote work)
- Clean desk and clear screen policies

#### Module 3: Access Control and Authentication (15 min)
- Password requirements and best practices
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) - mandatory for all accounts
- Password managers: approved tools and usage
- Never share credentials or leave accounts logged in
- Secure handling of privileged accounts (for applicable roles)
- Account lockout procedures and support contacts

#### Module 4: Email and Phishing Awareness (20 min)
- Recognizing phishing emails: red flags and indicators
- Common social engineering tactics
- How to verify sender authenticity
- Safe handling of email attachments and links
- Reporting suspicious emails using phishing report button
- Real-world phishing examples and case studies

#### Module 5: Data Protection and Privacy (15 min)
- Personal information and PII: definitions and examples
- Privacy principles: collection, use, disclosure, retention
- Lawful processing of union member data
- Data breach consequences and legal obligations
- Secure data storage and transmission
- Privacy by design principles

#### Module 6: Secure Remote Work (10 min - for remote workers)
- VPN usage: mandatory for all remote connections
- Home network security best practices
- Physical security of home office and devices
- Public Wi-Fi risks and precautions
- Protecting devices from theft or loss
- Separation of personal and work activities

#### Module 7: Incident Reporting (10 min)
- What is a security incident: definitions and examples
- Reporting procedures: who to contact, when, and how
- Incident reporting channels: security@unioneyes.com, incident hotline
- No blame culture: self-reporting encouraged
- Time-sensitive reporting: 4-hour requirement
- What happens after reporting: incident response process

### 5.3 Live Onboarding Session (30 min)
- Conducted by Security Team member within first 2 weeks
- Interactive Q&A session
- Demonstration of security tools (VPN, MFA, password manager)
- Device security setup walkthrough
- Meet the security team and understand support channels

### 5.4 Onboarding Assessment
- 15-question quiz covering all modules (minimum 80% passing score)
- Unlimited attempts permitted
- Remedial training for repeated failures
- Completion certificate issued and recorded in LMS

### 5.5 Consequences of Non-Completion
- **Day 30:** Manager notified of incomplete training
- **Day 35:** System access suspended (email remains active)
- **Day 40:** HR counseling and written reminder
- **Day 45:** Escalation to executive team; may result in termination during probation

## 6. Annual Security Refresher Training

### 6.1 Timing and Requirements
- **Frequency:** Annually (within 12 months of previous training completion)
- **Grace period:** 30-day grace period after anniversary date
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Delivery:** Online self-paced with interactive elements
- **Mandatory:** 100% completion required for all active personnel

### 6.2 Annual Training Content

#### Module 1: Security Landscape Update (15 min)
- Current threat landscape: emerging threats and attack trends
- Recent security incidents in industry (anonymized case studies)
- Union Eyes security posture: improvements and initiatives
- Regulatory changes and compliance updates

#### Module 2: Phishing and Social Engineering Advanced (15 min)
- Sophisticated phishing techniques: spear phishing, whaling, BEC
- Social engineering beyond email: phone calls (vishing), SMS (smishing)
- AI-generated phishing and deepfakes
- Real examples from Union Eyes phishing simulations (anonymized)
- Red team exercises and lessons learned

#### Module 3: Data Protection and Privacy Deep Dive (15 min)
- Privacy incidents and lessons learned
- Proper handling of union member PII and financial data
- Data minimization: collect only what you need
- Secure data disposal and retention
- Cross-border data transfers and legal considerations
- Privacy rights: responding to subject access requests

#### Module 4: Secure Development and Operations (15 min - for technical staff)
- Secure coding practices refresh
- Supply chain security and third-party risk
- Secrets management: never hardcode credentials
- Security testing and vulnerability management
- Incident lessons learned from technical incidents

#### Module 4 (Alternate): Security for Business Users (15 min - for non-technical staff)
- Secure document handling and sharing
- Protecting sensitive information in communications
- Vendor and partner security considerations
- Travel security best practices
- Physical security awareness

#### Module 5: Your Role in Security (10 min)
- Individual accountability and responsibility
- Security culture: everyone's job
- How to champion security within your team
- Resources and support available
- Policy updates and changes in past year

### 6.3 Annual Assessment
- 20-question quiz covering updated content (minimum 85% passing score)
- Two attempts permitted
- Mandatory remedial training for failures
- Completion tracked and reported to management

### 6.4 Tracking and Enforcement
- **60 days before due:** First reminder email
- **30 days before due:** Second reminder email
- **7 days before due:** Final reminder; manager notified
- **Due date:** Access suspension if not complete
- **30 days overdue:** HR disciplinary action

## 8. Phishing Simulations

### 8.1 Program Overview
- **Frequency:** Monthly simulated phishing campaigns
- **Participation:** Mandatory for all personnel with email accounts
- **Vendor:** [Approved phishing simulation platform, e.g., KnowBe4, Proofpoint]
- **Approach:** Progressive difficulty; no trick questions; educational focus

### 8.2 Simulation Scenarios
- Generic phishing emails (low difficulty)
- Spear phishing targeting roles or departments (medium difficulty)
- Clone phishing (legitimate email modified maliciously) (medium-high)
- Executive impersonation and BEC scenarios (high difficulty)
- Voice phishing (vishing) simulations (quarterly)
- SMS phishing (smishing) simulations (quarterly)

### 8.3 Simulation Metrics
- **Click rate:** Percentage clicking malicious link
- **Data entry rate:** Percentage entering credentials on fake site
- **Report rate:** Percentage reporting phishing using report button
- **Target:** <10% click rate; >50% report rate within 12 months

### 8.4 Response to Failures
- **First click:** Immediate just-in-time training (5-minute micro-learning)
- **Repeat clicks (3+ in 6 months):** Mandatory remedial training (30 minutes)
- **Persistent failures (5+ in 12 months):** Manager notified; individualized coaching
- **No penalties:** Educational approach; no disciplinary action for phishing clicks

### 8.5 Positive Reinforcement
- Recognition for reporting simulated phishing
- Quarterly "Security Champion" awards for high reporters
- Team-based metrics to encourage peer support
- Gamification: leaderboards, badges, certificates

### 8.6 Phishing Simulation Ethics
- No testing during high-stress periods (e.g., layoffs, major incidents)
- No use of legitimate HR, finance, or executive email addresses
- No overly sensitive subject lines (health, personal loss)
- Clear disclosure that simulations are ongoing (general awareness)
- Opt-out for employees experiencing harassment or safety concerns (with alternative training)

## 10. Specialized and Ad-Hoc Training

### 10.1 New Threat Training
When new significant threats emerge (zero-day vulnerabilities, major attack campaigns), security team deploys:
- Urgent security alert via email/Slack
- Short (5-10 minute) training video or document
- Specific protective actions required
- Live Q&A session if high impact
- Completion tracking for critical threats

### 10.2 Post-Incident Training
Following significant security incidents:
- Lessons learned session for affected teams
- Organization-wide training if broadly relevant
- Policy or procedure updates and training
- Remediation and prevention focus

### 10.3 Compliance Training
For regulatory or contractual requirements:
- SOC 2 audit preparation training
- GDPR and PIPEDA compliance updates
- Industry-specific training (e.g., PCI DSS if applicable)
- Third-party audit support training

### 10.4 Executive Security Briefings
**Frequency:** Quarterly
**Audience:** C-suite, VP level, board members
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Content:**
- Threat landscape for executives (business email compromise, social engineering)
- Industry threat intelligence
- Organizational security metrics and KPIs
- Major security incidents (internal and industry)
- Strategic security initiatives and investments
- Regulatory and compliance updates
- Executive role in security culture

## 12. Training Completion Tracking and Reporting

### 12.1 Tracking Metrics
- Training enrollment rates
- Training completion rates by deadline
- Assessment pass rates
- Time to complete training
- Overdue training list
- Completion by department/team
- Phishing simulation metrics

### 12.2 Reporting Cadence
- **Weekly:** Manager reports on team members' overdue training
- **Monthly:** CSO dashboard: organization-wide completion rates, phishing metrics
- **Quarterly:** Executive report: comprehensive training metrics, trends, issues
- **Annually:** Board report: program effectiveness, year-over-year comparison

### 12.3 Completion Targets
- **Onboarding training completion:** 100% within 30 days
- **Annual refresher completion:** 100% by due date (30-day grace)
- **Role-based training completion:** 100% by assigned deadline
- **Phishing report rate:** >50% within 12 months
- **Phishing click rate:** <10% within 12 months

### 12.4 Red/Amber/Green (RAG) Status
- **Green:** >95% completion on time
- **Amber:** 85-95% completion or minor delays
- **Red:** <85% completion or significant delays

## 13. Enforcement and Consequences

### 13.1 Non-Completion Consequences
Training completion is mandatory and non-negotiable:

**Employees:**
- **Access suspension:** After deadline + grace period, access to systems suspended until training complete
- **Performance impact:** Training completion factored into performance reviews
- **Disciplinary escalation:** Persistent non-completion may result in written warning or termination

**Contractors:**
- **Access suspension:** Immediate upon deadline
- **Contract compliance:** Non-completion may constitute breach of contract
- **Contract non-renewal:** Chronic non-compliance factors into renewal decisions

**Managers:**
- **Accountability:** Managers responsible for team compliance
- **Manager metrics:** Team completion rates visible to management chain
- **Leadership expectation:** 100% team compliance expected

### 13.2 Exceptions
Exceptions require written approval from CSO:
- Medical leave or family emergency
- Extended leave of absence
- Technical issues preventing completion (must be documented)
- Reasonable accommodations for disabilities
