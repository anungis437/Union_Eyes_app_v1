# Information Security Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** February 2026  
**Owner:** Security & Compliance Team  
**Approver:** Executive Management  
**Review Schedule:** Annual (or upon significant change)

## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the overarching information security management system (ISMS) policy for Union Eyes. It defines management commitment, scope, objectives, and governance requirements to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All Union Eyes personnel (employees, contractors, and authorized third parties)
- All information assets (data, software, services, infrastructure, and documentation)
- All environments (production, staging, development, and testing)
- All access methods (web, API, administrative consoles, and integrations)

## 3. Management Commitment

Executive management commits to:

- Establish, maintain, and continually improve the ISMS
- Provide resources necessary to implement and sustain security controls
- Define security objectives aligned with business goals and legal obligations
- Enforce compliance with this policy and supporting standards
- Review ISMS performance and risk posture at least quarterly

## 4. Information Security Objectives

Union Eyes maintains measurable objectives that are reviewed quarterly and include:

- Reduce security incident response times and improve containment rates
- Maintain availability and resiliency targets defined in continuity plans
- Ensure access control coverage across all production services and data stores
- Achieve timely remediation of critical vulnerabilities
- Sustain compliance with applicable regulations and contractual obligations

## 5. Policy Principles

The ISMS is based on the following principles:

- **Risk-Based Security**: Controls are selected and prioritized based on risk assessment
- **Least Privilege**: Access is granted only as required for job responsibilities
- **Defense in Depth**: Multiple layers of control protect against single points of failure
- **Accountability**: Security responsibilities are documented and enforced
- **Continuous Improvement**: Controls are evaluated and improved through audits and metrics

## 6. Governance and Responsibilities

- **Executive Management**: Approves this policy, sets objectives, and reviews ISMS performance
- **Security & Compliance Team**: Owns ISMS operations, risk management, and policy maintenance
- **Engineering Leadership**: Ensures secure design, implementation, and operational controls
- **All Personnel**: Comply with policies, complete training, and report security incidents

## 7. Risk Management

- Maintain an information security risk assessment and treatment process
- Review risks at least annually and upon significant changes to systems or operations
- Document risk acceptance decisions and approvals

## 8. Asset Management

- Maintain an asset register covering hardware, software, data, and services
- Assign owners and classification to all critical assets
- Review the asset register at least annually and after major changes

## 9. Supplier and Third-Party Security

- Evaluate supplier security posture before onboarding
- Maintain supplier risk assessments and security documentation
- Include security requirements, breach notification SLAs, and audit rights in contracts
- Review critical suppliers annually

## 10. Access Control

- Enforce role-based access control and least privilege
- Require strong authentication and MFA for privileged access
- Review access rights on a defined schedule and upon role changes

## 11. Secure Development and Change Management

- Follow secure development lifecycle practices
- Perform security reviews for material changes and new features
- Maintain configuration and change management controls for production systems

## 12. Incident Management

- Maintain an incident response plan with defined roles and escalation paths
- Track and resolve security incidents with documented lessons learned
- Notify affected parties and regulators per legal and contractual requirements

## 13. Business Continuity and Backup

- Maintain continuity and disaster recovery plans for critical services
- Test recovery procedures on a defined schedule
- Protect backups with access controls and encryption

## 14. Training and Awareness

- Provide mandatory security awareness training to all personnel
- Deliver role-based training for privileged or security-sensitive functions
- Maintain training records and completion tracking

## 15. Monitoring and Logging

- Log security-relevant events across systems and services
- Review logs for anomalies and investigate suspicious activity
- Retain logs in line with compliance and operational requirements

## 16. Compliance and Audit

- Comply with applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations
- Maintain evidence of control implementation and effectiveness
- Conduct internal and external audits as scheduled

## 17. Policy Communication and Acknowledgment

- This policy is communicated to all personnel and relevant third parties
- Personnel must acknowledge this policy during onboarding and annually thereafter
- Policy exceptions require written approval by Security & Compliance and Executive Management

## 18. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, contract termination, or legal action.
